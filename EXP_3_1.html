What this illustrates

Without an external electric field, molecular dipoles are randomly oriented, so the material has little or no net polarization.

When the dielectric is placed between capacitor plates, the electric field exerts a torque that tends to rotate dipoles into alignment. Nonpolar molecules can also become polarized by a slight displacement of positive and negative charge centers.

Polarization increases when field strength and polarizability increase, and it decreases when thermal agitation is large.

This animation is conceptual rather than microscopic: it is meant to show orientation + induced dipole formation inside the dielectric.
